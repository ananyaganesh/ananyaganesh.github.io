I am a second year Master's student at UMass Amherst, studying Computer Science and specializing in AI. I'm interested in Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing and Information Extraction.

Over the summer, I've been a research intern at the Information Extraction and Synthesis Laboratory (IESL) at UMass, supervised by Professor Andrew McCallum. I'm working on reducing core argument confusion in a neural model for Semantic Role Labeling called LISA. See here for more details!

During Spring 2018, I worked with Lexalytics as part of an independent study on improving sentiment analysis by resolving polysemous words. Our results on word sense induction were accepted to TextGraphs 2018: the Workshop on Graph-based Methods for Natural Language Processing at NAACL-HLT 2018! Check out the paper here, and a KD Nuggets article describing the method here.

Previously, I received my Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from Anna University, Chennai, India. I spent a brief summer at the Computational Linguistics Lab at Nara Institute of Science and Technology, Japan, where I worked on multi-word expressions with Professor Yuji Matsumoto.
